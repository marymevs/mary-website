Why I built Gist

How being offline turned into building a daily personal briefing — paper-forward, screen-minimizing, meant to make the phone a smaller part of the day.

// Draft in progress. The outline below sketches the shape; the full essay arrives when it arrives.
§ — Outline

Outline

  1. What I actually missed. Not what you'd think. Not the apps. The small daily signal — weather, schedule, who's around.

  2. The insight. You don't need a phone, you need a daily briefing. Most of what the phone gives you is the brief; the rest is noise.

  3. Why paper. Not anti-screen — pro-artifact. Paper has finite edges. It doesn't notify. You read it once and you're done.

  4. The thesis. Your day, on paper. Composed for you the night before, printed before you wake up, waiting on the kitchen counter.

  5. Where Gist is going. From morning briefing to operating system for a quieter day.
