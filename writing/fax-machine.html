Breaking up with my fax machine

Putting Gist on paper without a screen. Fax → Raspberry Pi + CUPS → email-to-print. Why the fax was the answer right up until it wasn't.

// Draft in progress. The outline below sketches the shape; the full essay arrives when it arrives.
§ — Outline

Outline

  1. Why fax. The original premise. One-way, paper-native, no UI to fight, no app to install. Almost suspiciously perfect.

  2. Where fax worked. The first weeks. The feeling of pulling a Gist out of a machine that doesn't ask anything of you.

  3. Where fax broke. Phaxio costs. Failed deliveries. The growing realization that the fax network is held together by trust I don't have.

  4. The Raspberry Pi + CUPS attempt. Putting the printer on the user's network. A small daemon, a Firebase Admin client, a queue, an lp call. Plumbing all the way down.

  5. Why email-to-print won. Every modern printer already speaks email. HP ePrint, Brother iPrint, Epson Email Print. The right transport was hiding in plain sight the whole time.

  6. What I learned about delivery. Same artifact, different transport. Substrate matters less than picture. The plumbing decision is rarely the brand decision.
