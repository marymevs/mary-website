What Waeve taught me

The DTC wig brand I co-founded, raised $2M for, scaled to nine people, and wound down. What it taught me about brand, building, and knowing when to stop.

// Draft in progress. The outline below sketches the shape; the full essay arrives when it arrives.
§ — Outline

Outline

  1. The premise. Wigs as a growth product, not a stigma. The bet underneath the bet.

  2. What worked. The 300 WaeveBaes. The brand-first approach. Co-founder fit forged before any capital arrived.

  3. What didn't. The custom-tech bet. The Glossier-CTO collaboration that didn't ship. The Shopify revert — not a retreat, the engineering call.

  4. Winding down. Why it was the right call, and why I'm proud of when we made it.

  5. The lessons. Brand and engineering aren't separate disciplines. Custom is expensive. Knowing when to stop is the leadership skill nobody teaches.
