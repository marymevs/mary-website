On narrative

Founding Waeve gave me something to write about. Until then I didn't realize the brand work I'd been doing was narrative work — I just hadn't named it.

// Draft in progress. The outline below sketches the shape; the full essay arrives when it arrives.
§ — Outline

Outline

  1. Before. Writing as something other people did. The years I spent assuming voice was a separate discipline from building.

  2. Brand as writing. The years of brand work I didn't realize were narrative work. Every line of copy, every product name, every founder interview — all of it was the same job.

  3. What founding taught me about voice. You can't fake the sentences. Only the people inside the company sound like the company.

  4. Why now, not before. You can't write yet about a thing you're still inside. Waeve had to end before I could see it.

  5. What narrative does that engineering can't. The seams an engineer files smooth are where the meaning lives.
