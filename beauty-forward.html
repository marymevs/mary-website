Beauty Forward

A donation logistics platform for beauty product redistribution, built for Isan Elba. Two production apps — donation pickup orchestration and AI-assisted inventory management — plus a shelter analytics dashboard in design.

Problem

Beauty Forward routes donated, unused beauty products from individuals to shelters. The operational reality has three pieces: getting product physically out of donors' homes (courier logistics, payment verification, scheduling), getting it onto shelter shelves with accurate inventory records (intake, classification, batching), and giving shelters visibility into what's coming and what they have on hand (analytics).

Three apps. Two in production. One in design.

Architecture

  • Angular 21
  • Firebase (Functions, Firestore, Data Connect)
  • Vertex AI · Gemini 1.5 Flash
  • Roadie
  • Givebutter
  • HubSpot
  • Resend

donation-delivery-app is the donor-facing pickup scheduler. Angular 21 frontend, Firebase Cloud Functions backend, Firestore for state. Integrates Roadie for last-mile courier dispatch, Givebutter for donation processing and payment verification, HubSpot for CRM upserts, and Resend for transactional email.

inventory-management-system is the shelter-facing intake and batching app. Same Angular + Firebase shape, plus Firebase Data Connect for relational queries and Vertex AI's Gemini 1.5 Flash for photo-to-inventory extraction at intake.

The analytics dashboard is the third app, currently in design. See "What's next" for the planned shape.

Technical decisions

  • Three-path payment + courier orchestration with webhook recovery

    donation-delivery-app/functions/src/services/dispatch.service.ts:26–100 gates courier dispatch on Givebutter payment verification with three fallback paths: verified → dispatch immediately; Givebutter unreachable or slow → mark awaiting_payment and rely on the webhook backstop; verification rejected → mark payment_verification_failed and send a recovery email. The webhook handler at donation-delivery-app/functions/src/index.ts:448–605 re-dispatches when payment confirms later. The whole design assumes the third-party APIs will be down, slow, or weird eventually — the platform keeps working anyway.

  • DST-aware pickup window construction

    donation-delivery-app/functions/src/providers/roadie-provider.ts:168–191 converts human-readable pickup windows ("9am–11am", "morning") into the ISO-8601 UTC timestamps Roadie's API expects. The conversion uses Intl.DateTimeFormat against America/New_York so daylight-saving transitions resolve correctly — not naive Date arithmetic. The same path rejects windows that have already passed, with a one-hour buffer to absorb clock skew between the client, Firebase, and Roadie.

  • Givebutter verification via email + paginated search

    donation-delivery-app/functions/src/services/givebutter.service.ts:69–191 originally tried session-ID-based payment verification, but Google Pay's iframe broke session continuity. The replacement fetches the donor's recent Givebutter transactions by email, paginates up to five pages of fifty, filters client-side against a 30-minute lookback window, and stops early at the cutoff. Defensive amount parsing accounts for three slightly different fields the Givebutter API exposes depending on payment method.

  • Idempotent email send across concurrent trigger paths

    Confirmation emails can fire from three places: the donor-submit callable, the Firestore onCreate trigger, and the Givebutter webhook recovery path. A sendEmailOnce() pattern in donation-delivery-app/functions/src/services/resend.service.ts stores confirmationEmailSentAt on the donation document and checks it before sending, so duplicate sends from concurrent triggers are impossible by construction — not merely unlikely.

  • Multimodal photo-to-inventory with schema-constrained Gemini

    inventory-management-system/functions/src/index.ts:219–287 calls Gemini 1.5 Flash with the donor's product photo, temperature 0.2 (low, near-deterministic), and a JSON-schema constraint covering a 30-entry product-type enum plus a high/medium/low confidence field. The prompt asks the model to omit fields it isn't sure about, and the confidence field drives a UI hint that asks for manual verification on low-confidence extractions. When photo extraction is uncertain, a fallback chain tries Open Beauty Facts → Open Food Facts → UPCitemdb → text-only Gemini, in that order.

What's next

  • App 3 — analytics dashboard. Designed around three quant-relevant features: a policy backtesting playground (replay historical donation flows under alternative batching and dispatch rules, compare outcomes); shelter-volume forecasting with confidence bands (per-region, per-category, simple SARIMA or Prophet baseline benchmarked against held-out MAE); and real-time streaming tiles via SSE for live donation-flow visibility. Stretch scope: an optimization visualizer for the shelter-batching allocation problem.
  • Shelter acceptance rule enforcement. The data model already stores per-shelter acceptedTypes, rejectedTypes, preferredBrands, and capacityPerBatch. The matching algorithm that filters incoming products against these rules during batch allocation is being built — today the inventory app allocates products manually.
  • Real Givebutter session creation. The current donation flow builds a Givebutter checkout URL; the longer-term shape is a server-side session-creation API call so the platform can carry session metadata end-to-end and tighten reconciliation.
